The Community Development Department or more synonymous among the general public as KEMAS is an extension of the Adult Education Division which was established in 1961, through cabinet paper No. 385/25/60 with a main focus on the program "Eradication of Illiteracy". By 1977, this movement to eradicate illiteracy was enhanced with the introduction of the concept of 'Functional Literacy' by UNESCO as an effort to make the movement to eradicate poverty as one of the strategies for developing rural communities.
